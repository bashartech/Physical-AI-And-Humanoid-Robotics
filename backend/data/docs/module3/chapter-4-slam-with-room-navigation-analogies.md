# Chapter 4: SLAM with room-navigation analogies

### Introduction
This chapter introduces Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), a fundamental capability for autonomous robots. The goal is to intuitively explain how robots build maps of unknown environments while simultaneously figuring out their own position within those maps, drawing analogies to how humans navigate new spaces.\n
### 4.1 Intuition: Getting Your Bearings in a New Room\n-   **Intuition:** Imagine walking into a completely dark, unfamiliar room with only a flashlight. As you move, you're trying to draw a mental map of the room AND figure out exactly where you are on that map at the same time. This is what SLAM does.\n-   **Method:** Introduce SLAM as the problem of concurrently building a map of an unknown environment and localizing the robot within that map.
